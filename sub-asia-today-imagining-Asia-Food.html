Food

What is your favourite 'Asian' food and what country does it originate from?

Today, 'Asian food' is everywhere. In every big city you will be able to find sushi, curry, noodles, yum cha, or chutney.

Just like the region it is named after, Asian food is a smorgasbord of cooking styles. This diversity is because of the range of environments represented across the geography of Asia.

In the island and peninsular worlds of the region, such as in Southeast Asia and Japan, the diet has evolved around rice and fish. This is because there are no substantial grasslands in these areas so there's very little tradition of shepherds raising herd animals.

In Central Asia, on the other hand, the meat of various herd animals is cooked with powerful spices that are designed to keep the body warm in harsh winters. This was crucial for nomadic life on the grassy steppes. Centuries of migration and then colonisation served to 'hybridise' ‐ or mix ‐ methods of cooking and food preparation.
