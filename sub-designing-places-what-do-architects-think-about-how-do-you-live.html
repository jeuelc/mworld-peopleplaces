How Do You Live?

How do you live? Do you live with your parents? A brother or a sister? Imagine if your grandparents moved into your home do you have enough space for them? What spaces would you build for them to make them feel a part of your home?

Architects need to understand what the place they are designing will be used for, and what the people who will be using it need. Is it a house? Is it a library or an art gallery? Or is it a public place? If the building is a library do you only need a space for books and a desk for librarians or do you also need a cafè, offices, places to read, places to chat and places to hear people give talks? Sometimes the list is short and sometimes the list is long.

What people want and need in a place is an important consideration for architects. They help their client to make this list and when it is complete it is called the 'brief'. It is from the brief that the architect begins the design process.
