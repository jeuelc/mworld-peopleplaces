The Architect's Tools

Different professions use different tools. A writer uses words and a builder might use a hammer. Architects use drawings and models to get their jobs done. These are the architect's tools. They are used to show people how the building or the place will work, how it goes together and to help themselves and others understand what it will feel like to be in the space.

Plan drawings show how different spaces relate to each other. 'Section' drawings cut through the building, showing you what is inside. 'Elevation' drawings are side views that tell you where the windows and doors are and what the building is made of. 'Perspective' drawings and images help us to imagine what it might be like to be in the space. Some drawings are made by hand and some are made by a computer from a virtual 3D model that the architect has created.

And finally, physical models are one of the best ways of understanding a project that is yet to be built - they help you to see the whole project in miniature!
