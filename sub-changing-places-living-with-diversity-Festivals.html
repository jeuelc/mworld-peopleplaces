For many immigrant groups, annual festivals are a great way for a community to show its culture to the broader society and celebrate its ethnic heritage. The Irish have St Patricks Day, the Chinese have Chinese New Year celebrations and West Indians in New York City have a colourful street parade that attracts more than two million spectators on the Labor Day holiday in September.

For people of Mexican origin, the Cinco de Mayo (Fifth of May) festivities are a celebration of Mexican heritage and pride. Cinco de Mayo is celebrated both in Mexico and in American cities that have large populations of Mexican immigrants and their descendants. The city of Denver runs a popular two-day festival called Celebrate Culture with events including a parade, Chihuahua dog races, a green chili cook-off and musical performances.
