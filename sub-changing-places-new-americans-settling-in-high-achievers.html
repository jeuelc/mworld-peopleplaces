High Achievers

 

America has some remarkable immigrant success stories. Russian immigrant Sergey Brin co-founded Google; Austrian immigrant Arnold Schwarzenegger became a champion bodybuilder, Hollywood action hero and Californias 38th Governor. Perhaps America's most famous immigrant is Albert Einstein, the German-born physicist who spent much of his later life living and working in the US.

Beyond these famous names, countless immigrants have also achieved a great deal, especially those who are highly skilled, have technical expertise and entrepreneurial drive. A 2008 US Government study found that immigrants are 30 percent more likely to start a business than people born in America. These businesses create jobs, attract consumers and can be a powerful force in revitalising struggling cities.
