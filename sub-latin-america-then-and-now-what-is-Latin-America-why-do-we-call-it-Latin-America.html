Why do we call it Latin America?

 
click here

For the Europeans, Latin America was first called the Indies because Columbus thought he had arrived to India! Later, having learnt they were not in Asia but a brand new continent, the Europeans called it the 'New World'.

The name 'America' is said to come from an Italian explorer called Amerigo Vespucci who made several voyages to the New World in the early 1500s. Once the Spanish began to extend their empire the region also came to be known as Spanish America.

As one of Spain's imperial rivals, though, it was the French who began calling it 'Latin' America in the 1860s, when Napoleon III tried to gain imperial control over Mexico. Today we still call it Latin America because the region's languages (Spanish, Portuguese and French) all stem from Latin.
