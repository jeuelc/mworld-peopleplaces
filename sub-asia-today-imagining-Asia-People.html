People

If there was one defining characteristic of the region we call Asia today, it would be 'diversity'.

There are lots of ethnic distinctions across the region. These distinctions are both between the nations of Asia and within them. In China, for example, there are 55 minority groups officially recognised by the government, such as the Tibetans and the Uighurs, as well as the dominant Han nationality. In Malaysia, ethnic Malays live alongside people of Chinese and Indian descent and many other ethnic groups. Even in Japan, which is often thought of as being a country that doesn't have a lot of ethnic and cultural diversity, there are ethnic minorities such as the Ainu.

Uneven development has created even greater degrees of diversity. Life in parts of Asia, such as in some areas of Mongolia, Afghanistan, or Nepal, looks remarkably similar to how things would have been a thousand years ago. But the lifestyle of people living in big cities like Shanghai, Singapore, Tokyo and Mumbai would not be out of place in New York or London.
