Ethnic media is media that is produced by and for a particular ethnic community. For immigrants, being able to watch TV programs, listen to radio stations or read newspapers in their native language is a valuable way to keep cultural ties. Ethnic media also tell stories that are relevant to a specific community, such as news about changes to immigration laws, for example.

Spanish-language media are growing enormously in the United States. In 2010 there were more than 50 million Latinos (a person who can trace their ancestry to Latin America, Spain or Portugal) living in the US thats double the number that were there in 1990! Given the growth of this market, newspapers, TV and radio have flourished. The Spanish-language television broadcaster 'Univision' has started to rival the four major English-language networks (ABC, NBC, CBS and FOX) in major cities!
