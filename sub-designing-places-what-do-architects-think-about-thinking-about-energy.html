Thinking cleverly about how we design and build can help us to use less energy during the construction process and in the long term.

Buildings use a lot of energy! But we can use the wind, the sun and the rain to help us save energy! We do this using 'passive design principles'. This means that we use the sun, the wind and the rain to help us lower the energy a building consumes. What would you do to reduce energy use in a building?

Another thing architects can do to create energy efficient buildings is to think about the types of materials they use. Insulation in the walls and the roof helps keep buildings at an even temperature. Double-glazed windows keep warm air in and reduce the heat from the sun in summer. There are also many recycled materials that can be used to make buildings.

Buildings stick around for a long time. If we can work out ways to minimise the energy we use it can be better for the whole planet.
