Singapore is a great example of a country that has emerged out of imperialism. Until the British East India Company established a settlement in 1819, only several hundred Malay fishermen and their families lived there. The island grew quickly after that. It was used by the British as an international seaport and a place used for rubber plantations, and British administrators worked with merchants to bring hundreds of thousands of Chinese and Indian workers.

Singapore declared independence from the United Kingdom in 1963, joining the Federation of Malaysia, then gained independence as the Republic of Singapore in 1965. By then it was ethnically diverse.

There was some mistrust among different communities. The government needed to create a coherent nation out of this melting pot. It seems to have worked by giving equal weight to each culture's right to practise its own traditions, so long as those traditions were not offensive to the other communities on the island.

Singapore has four official government languages, English, Malay, Tamil and Mandarin. And public holidays include festivals of all the prominent races and religions.

Imperialism

Imperialism is where a country increases its power by gaining control over other areas of the world. They claim foreign countries as part of their own nation, forming an empire.
