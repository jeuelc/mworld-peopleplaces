From Sketches to Technical Detail

At the start of the design process, drawings, models and perspective images are quite rough; they might be hand drawn and give a broad conceptual overview of the design.

But as the architect works with the client and the users of the space the drawings become more developed and detailed. Once the client is happy that the design has all the spaces they require the architect needs to incorporate important things such as how the building will stand up, how the materials go together, how much the building will cost and what rules and regulations need to be taken into account.

Architects collaborate with many other people such as engineers, building and interior designers and builders to work these things out. As they do, their designs develop. They become more detailed and technical until a builder can use them to construct the building. The drawings that an architect gives to a builder are called 'working drawings'.
