Latin America before Columbus

Did Christopher Columbus discover a 'New World' when he first touched American soil in 1492? Well, that's what the Europeans thought. But it was only 'new' to them. Hundreds of indigenous communities with a total population of several million had already been living in the Americas for centuries before Columbus's arrival. In fact, some of their cities were larger and more effectively organised than the cities of Europe.

One of these cities was Tenochtitlàn, which was the capital of the Mexica people, who we know today as the Aztecs. It was so huge and sophisticated the Europeans had never seen anything like it. Over the centuries, Mexico City has slowly been built on top of the ruins of Tenochtitlàn. And because Tenochtitlàn had been built on a lake, the soft soil beneath means that today the sheer weight of Mexico City makes it sink 10-15cm every year!
