Two way flows

Imagine the North American cowboy or the Argentine gaucho without a horse! Imagine Italian food without tomatoes!

Through colonisation, Europe brought horses to the Americas and took tomatoes back with them. During the colonial era, colonisation usually meant that one dominant, imperial country exerted control over another country and its people, which were usually far away. Their goal was to make profits by controlling the worlds trade networks. To do this, the imperial countries also forcefully tried to make colonised peoples more like them, to have them adopt their cultures and speak their languages.

But when two peoples come into contact the cultural flows occur in both directions. Between the Old World and the New, animals, foods, people, diseases and new ideas were exchanged. Europe introduced rice, onions, cows and the common cold to the Americas, while it received corn, potatoes, tobacco and turkeys in return!
