New Americans

Lets use the United States as a case study to explore in more detail what its like for immigrants to settle in a new land. The US is a great example as it is the classic nation of immigrants. It has more immigrants than any other country in the world. About 40 million immigrants live in America. To put this into context, if immigrants in America were a country on their own, its population size would be greater than that of 162 other countries in the world!

Immigrants in the United States are encouraged to hold on to their cultural traditions, which means America is a multicultural society. Immigrants in time can also become American citizens, making them new Americans.

The experiences of immigrants in settling in America are pretty varied but generally immigrants encounter a number of challenges as well as opportunities.
