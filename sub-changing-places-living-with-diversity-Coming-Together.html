Immigrants and native-born Americans mingle with each other at work, school and in social settings. The amount of cross-cultural interaction among Americans of different backgrounds and ethnic origins is one of the great strengths of that society.

One of the really fascinating outcomes of diversity in America is the growth in marriages between people of different ethnic groups and different faiths. Between 2000 and 2010, nearly 10% of all marriages across the country involved two people of different ethnic backgrounds. Since about the year 2000, 45% of all marriages involved either two religions or two Christian doctrines that conflicted with each other. Many people who marry across faith lines convert to the religion of their spouse.

Cross-cultural interaction is crucial for helping harmony and understanding, especially in a society as diverse as the United States.
