Putting It All Together

Architects have to think about how things go together. A building needs to stand up and provide shelter for the people who use it. Two ways to make a building stand up are by using strong materials, like bricks and stone, or to build carefully, with lots of pillars, posts and beams.

If you build with bricks and stone your building might look monumental. It will be heavy and weighty and this can make a building feel important. If you lived in a house like this it might make you feel cosy and secure. Can you think of a building near you that makes you feel this way?

Buildings made with timber and glass might look bright and airy. Perhaps you have a fantastic view that you want to look at all day. If you lived in a house like this you might feel like you were living in the landscape or maybe a fishbowl! This is called post and beam construction. It relies on posts or columns and beams to make it stand up. Often you can see these columns and beams when you walk through the building.
