When it comes to immigration, European countries are quite unusual. Citizens who are part of the European Union (EU) enjoy freedom of movement into other EU countries. So, for instance, a Polish citizen can live and work in Spain. In most parts of the world, governments control immigration very tightly. Yet in the case of the EU, it is a fundamental right that citizens of member countries can move freely within the Union.

Europe is also unusual in that it has changed from being a sender of migrants to countries such as the USA and Australia to being a recipient of them. Four per cent of the EU population were born outside the continent of Europe. In some countries, such as Austria, Sweden, Spain and the Netherlands, the number is much higher. There is some public resistance to the arrival of non-European immigrants; however governments in Europe see immigration as an opportunity to balance its ageing population.
