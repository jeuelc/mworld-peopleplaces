Asia's challenges in the 21st century are as varied as the countries that make up the region.

Many struggle with problems that stem from rapid industrialisation and development, such as pollution and environmental damage. In northern China, deforestation of a forest belt that used to capture the sand of the Gobi Desert means that cities like Beijing are often blanketed by smog and sandstorms. In Bangladesh, industrial waste dumped into rivers has caused severe threats to human health. In Pacific islands such as Fiji, rising sea levels threatens the very existence of whole communities.

Families relate to each other differently as communities change with new developments.

In many Asian countries, the norm for families is changing from traditionally large, multigenerational groups, to smaller families of just parents and their children.

Society is also changing from male dominated, to a more equal situation for men and women. Women have increased access to education and wealth, and a third of all new millionaires each year in China are women.
