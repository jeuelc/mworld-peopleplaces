While much of Asia is changing rapidly today, in some parts of the region things haven't changed much over the past few thousand years. In the plains of Mongolia, while several sophisticated urban settlements exist, more than a third of the population still live nomadic lifestyles, living in 'ger-teregs' (yurts on carts). Even those who live in the suburban fringes of cities choose to reside in yurts.

Mongolia doesn't get much rain and its climate is arid and hostile, so it's no surprise that it's the most sparsely populated country in the world! There are 2.9 million people spread over a region nearly 1.6 square million kilometres in size. Among nomadic communities, children often learn to ride on horseback before they can walk properly, and hunting with a bow and arrow at full gallop is an expected skill of young nomadic Mongols.
