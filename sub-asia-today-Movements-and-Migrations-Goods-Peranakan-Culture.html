How has your community changed over time? Ask your family members and teachers what it was like when they were your age.

Throughout history, all communities have changed over time due to cultural encounters with other communities. Cooking, dress, language, music or religion inevitably change as these encounters occur. Sometimes, though, when two communities encounter each other a completely new one is created! The Peranakan people of Southeast Asia are a great example of this.

Before the British and Dutch colonised what is today Malaysia and Indonesia, small pockets of traders from China had settled in the region. Over time, they intermarried with local women, and a unique hybrid culture emerged. Their clothes were influenced by a mix of Chinese and Malay Archipelago fashions, they developed their own dialect ‐ a fusion of Chinese words into a Malay language base ‐ and they developed their own cuisine that used curries and other spices with Chinese cooking styles. Today, Peranakan food is one of the most celebrated forms of cuisine in Southeast Asia, a must-try for tourists and a favourite among non-Peranakan locals.
