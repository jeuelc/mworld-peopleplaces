Settling In

 
click here

For many immigrants, the early years of settlement can be challenging. Immigrants who don't speak English well may struggle to find a job, or be relegated to menial work, that is, work that is very different, less prestigious and well paid compared to what they did at home.

A lot of immigrants find that their qualifications and work experience from back home count for little in America. So to make a living they may need to accept jobs that are well below their qualifications and skill level. For example, it would be like being a doctor in your home country and having to drive a taxi.

Some immigrants also experience discrimination and endure poverty because of their background. Discrimination is the unfair treatment a person or group of people differently. For example, the 2011 US Census estimated that nearly one in four Mexican and Central American immigrant families in Los Angeles lived in poverty.

Still, many immigrants accept these initial hurdles and make sacrifices so that their children and grandchildren have more opportunities than they did.
