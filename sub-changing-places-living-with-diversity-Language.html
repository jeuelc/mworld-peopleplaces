Language is a central part of culture. Immigrants from non-English-speaking backgrounds may continue to speak in their mother tongue after settling in America. Children of immigrants may also take classes to learn the language of their ancestors as a way of keeping a connection between generations.

In 2011, nearly 38 million Americans spoke Spanish. Other popular foreign languages include Chinese, Hindi/Urdu, French, Vietnamese and the Tagalog language of the Philippines.

Minority languages add a lot to the richness of a society. However, the use of foreign languages in public spaces (such as state schools and in shops) has been controversial in the United States. Critics worry that the rise of foreign languages will lead to a decline in the use of English in the US. Some people wonder how people can communicate if they dont all speak the same language.
